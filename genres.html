Sub-Categories of Rock


What is Rock?
Rock music is a genre of popular music that originated as "rock and roll" in the United States in the 1950s, and developed into a range of different styles in the 1960s and later. It has its roots in 1940s' and 1950s' rock and roll, itself heavily influenced by blues, rhythm and blues and country music. Rock music also drew strongly on a number of other genres such as electric blues and folk, and incorporated influences from jazz, classical and other musical sources.




Rock is a very broad genre as it stems so many sub-categories all of which categorized through use of instruments used and/or the lyrics used in the song. Very often rock bands do not fit into just one category and may very often reach into various sub-genres with each song. The three most prolific sub-genres stemmed from the rock 'movement' are subjectively Punk Rock, Rock N' Roll, Pop Rock and of course Hard Rock.


Punk Rock

Developed in the early to mid-1970s, Punk rock bands rejected perceived excesses of mainstream 1970s rock.They typically use short or fast-paced songs, with hard-edged melodies and singing styles, stripped-down instrumentation, and often political, anti-establishment lyrics.

Rock N' Roll

Originated and evolved during the late 1940s and early 1950s. Classic rock and roll is usually played with one or two electric guitars (one lead, one rhythm), a double bass or string bass or (after the mid-1950s) an electric bass guitar, and a drum kit.

Pop Rock

This genre of music has a lighter, smoother approach that is more reminiscent of commercial pop music. Originating in the 1950s as an alternative to rock N' roll, early pop rock was influenced by the beat, arrangements, and style of rock and roll with greater emphasis on professional songwriting and recording craft.

The four core instruments in hard rock are the bass guitar, vocals, drums and the electric guitar.
